Course II · The Present

The Black Airbender system

Devon isn't teaching box breathing or "calm down" breathing. He teaches breath as an internal pressure system — a driver of movement, awareness and performance. Six phases, one arc:

1 Entry2 Pressure3 Rhythm 4 Movement5 Spiral6 Stillness
Pressure Exhale
Inhale naturally through the nose. Exhale longer, against slight resistance, abdomen gently engaged. Why it works: longer exhales raise CO₂ tolerance — and better CO₂ tolerance means better oxygen delivery (the Bohr effect). The oxygen was never the problem.
Pulse Breathing
Smooth intake; exhale in rhythmic pulses. Rhythm over count, awareness over control. Why it works: the nervous system entrains to rhythm — brain-body sync rises, vagal tone rises.
Temple Activation
Warm the hands, place on temples, breathe slow with long exhales. Why it works: stimulates trigeminal and vagal pathways — head tension, eye strain and mental fatigue ease off.
CO₂ toleranceLonger exhales → higher CO₂ tolerance → better oxygen delivery where it's needed (Bohr effect).
Vagus nerveLong exhales shift the system parasympathetic — heart rate down, calm and focus up.
Fascia & pressureBreath creates internal pressure waves; fascia responds to pressure, not just stretch.
VestibularSpiral movement + breath = balance, spatial awareness, multi-plane coordination.
